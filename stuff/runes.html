My runes orthography is mostly based off of Stella’s orthography, but simplified and modified a bit. Note that I generally prefer angular runes but the font which I am using has some rounded runes. There is not much I am able to do about this because the other fonts offered on that website do not have all the runes I want (ᚡ and other dotted runes, for example).

Consonants

  • ᛈ - /pʰ/ (pan)
  • ᛒ - /b/ (bat)
  • ᛏ - /tʰ/ (tin)
  • ᛞ - /d/ (dance)
  • ᛣ - /kʰ/ (call)
  • ᚸ - /ɡ/ (goal)
  • ᛗ - /m/ (mom)
  • ᚠ - /f/ (fall)
  • ᚡ - /v/ (vole)
  • ᚦ - /θ/ (thought)
  • ᚧ - /ð/ (that), also “the”
  • ᛋ - /s/ (sky), /z/ (thorns, only as plural marker or possessive marker)
  • ᛉ - /z/ (zoo)
  • ᛋᚳ - /ʃ/ (ship)
  • ᛉᚳ - /ʒ/ (vision)
  • ᚳ - /t͡ʃʰ/ (chat)
  • ᚷ - /d͡ʒ/ (joke)
  • ᚻ - /h/ (hand)
  • ᚺ - /x/ (loch, Bach)
  • ᚱ - /ɹ/ (room)
  • ᛚ - /l/ (light)
  • ᛡ - /j/ (yes)
  • ᚹ - /w/ (win)

Vowels

  • ᛇ - kit (/ɪ/)
  • ᛖ - dress (/ɛ/)
  • ᚫ - trap, bath (/æ/)
  • ᚪ - lot, cloth, thought, palm (/ɑ/)
  • ᚢ - strut (/ʌ/)
  • ᚤ - foot (/ʊ/)
  • ᛁ - fleece, happy (/i/)
  • ᛄ - face (/eɪ/)
  • ᛟ - goat (/oʊ/)
  • ᚥ - goose (/u/)
  • ᛠ - price (/aɪ/)
  • ᛟᛇ - choice (/ɔɪ/)
  • ᚫᚤ - mouth (/aʊ/)
  • ᚣ - new (/ju/)

The schwa should be written with whatever vowel it would be if it was “stressed”. If that is not possible, use ᚢ. For example, the word “photograph” is written ᚠᛟᛏᛟᚸᚱᚫᚠ, not ᚠᛟᛏᚢᚸᚱᚫᚠ because the word “photo” is written and pronounced ᚠᛟᛏᛟ.

Spelling exceptions

Not every word is spelled exactly, however. There are some where I’ve decided to deviate from my own system.
  • “blew” - ᛒᛚᚥ
  • “blue” - ᛒᛚᚣ (an old pronunciation of this word was /bljuː/
  • “to” - ᛏᚤ
  • “to” - ᛏᚥ
  • “the” - ᚧ (because this is an extremely common word)
  • “two” - ᛏᚹᚥ (an old pronunciation of this word was /twuː/)

Punctuation

  • ᛫ - word separator
  • ᛬ - full stop, end of sentence
  • ᛭ - question mark
    • ᛭ᚹᚢᛏ᛫ᛇᛉ᛫ᚣᚱ᛫ᚾᛄᛗ᛬
      What is your name?

Sample Text (The North Wind and the Sun)

Note: There are probably mistakes everywhere in this.

ᚧ᛫ᚾᛟᚱᚦ᛫ᚹᛇᚾᛞ᛫ᚫᚾᛞ᛫ᚧ᛫ᛋᚢᚾ᛫ᚹᚢᚱ᛫ᛞᛇᛋᛈᚣᛏᛇᛝ᛫ᚹᛇᚳ᛫ᚹᚢᛉ᛫ᛋᛏᚱᚪᛝᚸᚱ᛫᛫ᚹᛖᚾ᛫ᛄ᛫ᛏᚱᚫᚡᛚᚱ᛫ᚳᛄᛗ᛫ᚢᛚᚪᛝ᛫ᚱᚫᛈᛞ᛫ᛇᚾ᛫ᛄ᛫ᚹᛟᚱᛗ᛫ᚳᛚᛟᛣ᛬ The North Wind and the Sun were disputing which was the stronger, when a traveler came along wrapped in a warm cloak.
ᚧᛄ᛫ᚢᚸᚱᛁᛞ᛫ᚧᚫᛏ᛫ᚧ᛫ᚹᚢᚾ᛫ᚻᚢ᛫ᚠᚢᚱᛋᛏ᛫ᛋᚢᛣᛋᛁᛞᛖᛞ᛫ᛇᚾ᛫ᛗᛄᛣᛇᛝ᛫ᚧ᛫ᛏᚱᚫᚡᛚᚱ᛫ᛏᛄᛣ᛫ᚪᚠ᛫ᚻᛇᛉ᛫ᚳᛚᛟᛣ᛫ᛋᚳᚤᛞ᛫ᛒᛁ᛫ᚳᚢᚾᛋᛇᛞᚱᛞ᛫ᛋᛏᚱᚪᚾᚸᚱ᛫ᚧᚫᚾ᛫ᚧ᛫ᚢᚧᚱ᛬ They agreed that the one who first succeeded in making the traveler take his cloak off should be considered stronger than the other.
ᚧᛖᚾ᛫ᚧ᛫ᚾᛟᚱᚦ᛫ᚹᛇᚾᛞ᛫ᛒᛚᚥ᛫ᚫᛉ᛫ᚻᚪᚱᛞ᛫ᚫᛉ᛫ᚻᛁ᛫ᛣᚤᛞ᛫᛫ᛒᚢᛏ᛫ᚧ᛫ᛗᛟᚱ᛫ᚻᛁ᛫ᛒᛚᚥ᛫ᚧ᛫ᛗᛟᚱ᛫ᛣᛚᛟᛋᛚᛁ᛫ᛞᛇᛞ᛫ᚧ᛫ᛏᚱᚫᚡᛚᚱ᛫ᚠᛟᛚᛞ᛫ᚻᛇᛉ᛫ᛣᛚᛟᛣ᛫ᚢᚱᚫᚤᚾᛞ᛫ᚻᛇᛗ᛬ Then the North Wind blew as hard as he could, but the more he blew the more closely did the traveler fold his cloak around him;
ᚫᚾᛞ᛫ᚫᛏ᛫ᛚᚫᛋᛏ᛫᛫ᚧ᛫ᚾᛟᚱᚦ᛫ᚹᛇᚾᛞ᛫ᚸᛄᚡ᛫ᚢᛈ᛫ᚧ᛫ᚢᛏᛖᛗᛈᛏ᛬ᚧᛖᚾ᛫ᚧ᛫ᛋᚢᚾ᛫ᛋᚳᛠᚾᛞ᛫ᚫᚤᛏ᛫ᚹᚪᚱᛗᛚᛁ᛫᛫ᚫᚾᛞ᛫ᛇᛗᛁᛞᛁᛖᛏᛚᛁ᛫ᚦ᛫ᛏᚱᚫᚡᛚᚱ᛫ᛏᚤᛣ᛫ᚪᚠ᛫ᚻᛇᛉ᛫ᚳᛚᛟᛣ᛬ and at last the North Wind gave up the attempt. Then the Sun shined out warmly, and immediately the traveler took off his cloak.
ᚫᚾᛞ᛫ᛋᛟ᛫ᚧ᛫ᚾᛟᚱᚦ᛫ᚹᛇᚾᛞ᛫ᚹᚢᛉ᛫ᚢᛒᛚᛠᚷᛞ᛫ᛏᚤ᛫ᛣᚢᚾᚠᛖᛋ᛫ᚧᚫᛏ᛫ᚧ᛫ᛋᚢᚾ᛫ᚹᚢᛉ᛫ᚧ᛫ᛋᛏᚱᚪᚾᚸᚱ᛫ᚢᚡ᛫ᚧ᛫ᛏᚹᚥ᛬ And so the North Wind was obliged to confess that the Sun was the stronger of the two.
